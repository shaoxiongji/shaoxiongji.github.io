About


Dr. Shaoxiong Ji is a principal investigator at ELLIS Institute Finland and an assistant professor at University of Turku, Finland. He is also a Foundation PS Fellow and a faculty member of ELLIS Society. Prior to this, he was an independent research group leader at Technical University of Darmstadt, Germany, and a postdoctoral researcher at University of Helsinki, working on high-performance language technology with Prof. Jörg Tiedemann. He did his PhD at Aalto University, under the supervision of Assoc. Prof. Pekka Marttinen. During his doctoral candidature, he was a visiting researcher with Prof. Hinrich Schütze at the University of Munich (LMU Munich, Germany) and with Dr. Mikko Peltola at the Finnish Institute for Health and Welfare (THL, Finland). Prior to his doctoral candidature, he did MPhil research with Prof. Xue Li and Prof. Helen Zi Huang at the University of Queensland (UQ Australia), visiting research with Assoc. Prof. Erik Cambria at Nanyang Technological University (NTU Singapore), and research assistant and visit research with Dr. Guodong Long and Dr. Shirui Pan at University of Technology Sydney (UTS Australia).

Research Interest

Machine Learning: Deep Learning, Federated Learning, Multitask and Meta Learning
Natural Language Processing: Affective Computing, Information Extraction, Language Modeling, Multilingualism
Applications: Health Informatics, Social Text Analytics, Clinical Notes, Mental Health, Time Series

LLM Post-Training Techniques: Investigating and developing advanced techniques for fine-tuning, reasoning, alignment, LLM agents, parameter-efficient fine-tuning, and knowledge injection for pre-trained LLMs to enhance their performance and safety.

Multilingualism in LLMs: Researching methods, e.g., continual pre-training, finetuning, and inference, to improve LLM performance for diverse languages, including low-resource languages.

AI for Healthcare: Designing, implementing, and evaluating autonomous LLM powered agents for tasks such as clinical decision support, medical information retrieval, patient education, virtual assistants, and workflow automation in healthcare. Addressing critical considerations related to fairness, bias, explainability, and privacy of LLMs in healthcare and social good applications.

Multimodality in LLMs: Developing and evaluating multimodal LLMs that can integrate and reason over various data types. In healthcare, they include clinical text, medical images (X-rays, MRIs), and electronic health records (EHRs).
